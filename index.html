Hardware currently used
  • Automatic doors in buildings, commonly seen in hospitals for people with wheelchairs, open to make space. Open upon detection of movement Remote controlled doors, Doors that open/close on command at the push of a trigger. Can be operated by a human, or more commonly by an AI
  • Motion sensor lights Lighting Cove lighting, path casts a soft glow for gentle places like yoga studios. Linear LEDs, surface lighting in gyms, used to create the effect of going lightspeed through the universe. Natural lighting from the sun, allows for energy to be saved.Timers for lights, decides when to shut on and off lights, typically turned off at night when everyone leaves.
  • PCs with a good graphics card. They need it since most, if not all architects use 3D rendering tools to create blueprints of their building ideas. Decent specs to go along with said pc, so that the GPU is utilized to its fullest, and isn't in any way bottle capped
  • 3d printing a model of a design, brings 3d renders even more to life. 3D printed houses, to go along with their 3D printed building, so that project/city managers realize how much space their building is going to take. 3d printed bridges, used in scenarios where there isn’t enough time to build a bridge with stone/wood. Used in wars to get around rough terrain, and to make dull bridges pop out, like MX3D bridge constructed in Amsterdam
  • There currently exists windows that change colors by an electronic signal. This allows for better privacy in skyscrapers, without the need to have obtuse curtains. This tech can be used to add further lighting to a building for an event
  • Cameras are placed everywhere to insure security, scares off criminals when they know someone is watching them, calls police automatically when suspicious activity is seen RFID chips, in phones/credit cards, even in people’s hands to open up doors, buy things. Typically used in offices
